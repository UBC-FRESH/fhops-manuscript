Dear SoftwareX Editors,

On behalf of my coauthors, I am pleased to submit the manuscript titled
"FHOPS: a reproducible optimisation and benchmarking stack for forest
harvest operations" for consideration as an Original Software Publication in
SoftwareX.

FHOPS is an open-source Python platform for reproducible forest harvest
operations planning studies. It provides a shared scenario contract,
mixed-integer and heuristic solver pathways, and a unified evaluation layer
for schedule quality, robustness, and cost interpretation. The manuscript
documents FHOPS v1.0.0 as a stable software release and presents a
reproducible benchmark evidence package generated from versioned manuscript
assets.

We believe the manuscript is a good fit for SoftwareX because the primary
contribution is reusable research software rather than a new optimization
theorem or a single case-study result. FHOPS addresses a recurring gap in
forest operations research: planning tools are often site-specific, difficult
to audit, and hard to compare across studies. By publishing the software,
metadata, benchmark workflow, and source package together, this submission
provides an inspectable baseline that other researchers can reuse and extend.

The software is publicly available under the MIT License:

- Source repository: https://github.com/UBC-FRESH/fhops
- Versioned release: https://github.com/UBC-FRESH/fhops/releases/tag/v1.0.0
- PyPI package: https://pypi.org/project/fhops/1.0.0/
- Documentation: https://ubc-fresh.github.io/fhops/

This manuscript is original, has not been published previously, and is not
under consideration elsewhere. All authors have approved the submitted
manuscript. The authors declare no competing financial interests or personal
relationships that could have appeared to influence the work reported in this
paper.
